CONSENT TO PARTICIPATE IN RESEARCH


Judging the Acceptability of Sentences

You are asked to participate in a research study conducted by Adam Royer (Principal Investigator) from the Linguistics Department. The purpose of the study is to investigate how acceptable native speakers of English find different sentences. You were selected as a possible participant in this study because you are 18 years of age or older and are a native English speaker.


Procedures

If you agree to participate in this study, you will listen to a series of sentences and be asked how acceptable each is to you. You may also be asked comprehension questions about the sentences. Participation in this study is expected to take approximately 30-45 minutes.


Potential Risks and Discomforts

None.


Potential Benefits to Subjects and/or to Society

There is no direct benefit to the subject from participation. Society may benefit from greater scientific knowledge about speech and human language which has consequences for language teaching, treatment for speech pathology, and the way computer technology is implemented in our everyday lives.


Payment for Participation

If you are participating in connection with a psychology or linguistics class and have signed up using the UCLA Psychology subject pool (SONA), you will receive 1 point of course credit that you signed up for. Otherwise, you will receive a cash payment at the rate of $10/hour.


Confidentiality

When you are finished with the experiment, you have the right to tell us to erase your responses you gave during the study. Otherwise, your responses will be retained indefinitely and may be shared with other researcher involved in this project or other subsequent projects. The personal information collected about you, such as your age, gender, and native language, may be described. However, such information will appear anonymously and the fact that you participated in this study will remain confidential.


Participation and Withdrawal

Participation is completely voluntary and it is your choice to stop participation at any point in the study, without penalty of any kind. You may refuse to answer any questions that you do not want to answer and still remain in the study.


Identification of Investigators

If you have any questions or concerns about the research, please feel free to contact the primary researcher, Adam Royer, Linguistics department (3125 Campbell Hall), UCLA, ajroyer@ucla.edu.


Rights of Research Subjects

You may withdraw your consent at any time and discontinue participation without penalty. You are not waiving any legal claims, rights or remedies because of your participation in this research study. If you have questions about your rights as a research subject, or you have concerns or suggestions and you want to talk to someone other than the researchers, you may contact the UCLA OHRPP by phone: (310) 206-2040 or by email: participants@research.ucla.edu
